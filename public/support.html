SteerPack for Jira: support and documentation

: support and documentation

Getting started

1. Install SteerPack from the Atlassian Marketplace into your Jira site. If you also want packs published to Confluence, install it into Confluence on the same site when prompted.

2. Open SteerPack from the Jira Apps menu.

3. Create your program, add its workstreams, and map each workstream to Jira projects and, if you use them, epics.

4. Add your milestones with baseline dates. Forecast dates and an optional start date can be set per milestone.

5. Set your reporting thresholds if the defaults do not suit (schedule variance and blocked-item counts that propose Amber and Red).

6. Generate your first pack. SteerPack is designed for a first pack in under fifteen minutes.

A clearly labelled sample program is available for empty sites so you can see a finished pack before connecting your own data. Removing it is one click, and it is never mixed with your data.

How the pack works

• Generating a pack takes an immutable snapshot of your Jira delivery data and PM assessments at that moment. Packs never change after generation; a new cycle means a new pack.

• The RAG status per workstream is proposed by deterministic rules (schedule variance against baseline, blocked items, overdue actions). A project manager can override the proposal, but only with a written reason, and the pack shows it as PM assessed.

• From your second pack onward, the pack shows movement since the previous pack: status changes, new risks, and milestones that slipped further.

• The pack narrative is templated from your data. The same data always produces the same words.

Outputs

• Confluence page: set the destination space and parent page in pack settings, then publish. Publishing runs as you, so the page is attributed to you and honours your Confluence permissions. The PowerPoint insert is attached to the page automatically; if attachment fails, the page still publishes and an "Attach insert to page" control lets you retry.

• One-page A4 PDF: open the pack view and print. Use A4 portrait at 100% scale. The pack is designed to hold one page; if a very large program overflows, the screen says so plainly rather than shrinking the content.

• PowerPoint insert: a fixed two-slide 16:9 editable insert, generated inside Atlassian and downloadable from the pack view. It drops into your own deck as native editable slides. If a large program's full status board cannot fit on slide 1, the slide shows the highest-severity workstreams and says so; the A4 pack always carries the full board.

Your data

• SteerPack runs entirely on Atlassian infrastructure. Your program data is stored in Forge storage inside your Atlassian cloud and never leaves it.

• The app makes no external network calls of any kind: no third-party services, no analytics, no tracking, no external fonts.

• Jira and Confluence access runs as the person using the app, so SteerPack can never show or publish anything the person could not see or do themselves.

• Uninstalling the app removes access to its stored data per Atlassian's Forge storage lifecycle.

Known limitations

• One program per site in this release, with up to ten workstreams per pack.

• Register displays are capped to keep the pack to one page (the nearest milestones, risks and decisions); the caps are always stated on the page, and totals reflect what Jira holds.

• If two administrators save the same program at the same instant, the later save can overwrite the earlier one. Ordinary concurrent editing is protected by a version check; this narrow case is documented and being tracked.

• Scheduled (unattended) pack generation is not in this release. Packs are generated on demand.
